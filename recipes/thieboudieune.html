Description

Thiéboudiène, also written thiéboudienne, thiépbou dieune, or sometimes tiep bou djen, is a traditional dish made with rice and fish, emblematic of Senegalese cuisine.

Often abbreviated as tieb, it is commonly called ceebu jën (literally, in Wolof, "fish rice") in Senegal. It is also called tiep in Mali.
